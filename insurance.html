We believe families deserve clarity about costs upfront. Here's everything you need to know about how we handle fees and insurance.

We Are Private Pay

We are currently private pay and do not bill insurance directly. Payment is due at the time of service, and we accept all major credit cards, HSA/FSA cards, and checks.

Being private pay allows us to focus entirely on your child's needs — without insurance-driven constraints on session length, frequency, or treatment approach. We get to do what's actually best for your family.

receipt_long

Superbill for Out-of-Network

We can provide a superbill — a detailed receipt with the billing codes your insurance company needs — that you may submit for potential out-of-network (OON) reimbursement. Many families are surprised by how much their plan covers.

Before your first session, we recommend asking your insurer:

  • help_outline Do I have out-of-network occupational therapy benefits?
  • help_outline What is my out-of-network deductible, and has it been met?
  • help_outline What percentage of the allowed amount will be reimbursed?
  • help_outline Do I need a referral or prior authorization for OON OT services?
savings

HSA & FSA Welcome

Occupational therapy is generally an eligible expense under Health Savings Accounts (HSA) and Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA). We accept HSA and FSA cards directly, making it easy to use pre-tax dollars for your child's care.

We recommend confirming eligibility with your HSA/FSA provider, as rules can vary by plan.

Common Questions

Why don't you accept insurance directly? expand_more

Accepting insurance directly requires us to justify every session with diagnostic codes, adhere to insurance-approved treatment limits, and spend significant time on administrative overhead. By being private pay, we can offer flexible session lengths, focus on your child's whole developmental picture, and spend our energy on care — not paperwork. We believe this results in better outcomes for families.

How do I submit a superbill to my insurance? expand_more

After each session (or monthly, if preferred), we'll provide a superbill with all the information your insurer needs — CPT codes, diagnosis codes, provider NPI, and service dates. You submit this directly to your insurance company, either online through their member portal, by mail, or by fax. Your insurer will then process it and reimburse you according to your out-of-network benefits.

What payment methods do you accept? expand_more

We accept all major credit and debit cards, HSA and FSA cards, and checks. Payment is due at the time of service. If you have questions about payment arrangements or financial considerations, please reach out — we're happy to have that conversation.

What is your cancellation policy? expand_more

We ask for at least 24 hours' notice for cancellations. Late cancellations or no-shows may be subject to a cancellation fee. We understand that life with kids is unpredictable — if something comes up, please reach out as soon as possible and we'll do our best to work with you.
